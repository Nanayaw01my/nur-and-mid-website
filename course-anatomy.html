1.3 Four Primary Tissue Types

  • Epithelial: Covering, lining, glandular. Simple (single layer) vs stratified (multiple). Shapes: squamous, cuboidal, columnar.
  • Connective: Loose (areolar, adipose), dense (tendons, ligaments), specialized (bone, cartilage, blood).
  • Muscle: Skeletal (striated, voluntary), Cardiac (striated, involuntary), Smooth (non-striated, involuntary).
  • Nervous: Neurons (conduct impulses), Neuroglia (support).

2. Skeletal System — Structure & Joints

2.1 Divisions: Axial vs Appendicular

  • Axial (80 bones): Skull, vertebral column (33 vertebrae), rib cage (12 pairs). Protects brain, spinal cord, heart, lungs.
  • Appendicular (126 bones): Upper/lower limbs, pectoral girdle (clavicle, scapula), pelvic girdle (hip bones).

2.2 Bone Types & Classification

2.3 Joint Classification

  • Fibrous (synarthroses): Immovable. Sutures of skull, gomphosis (tooth socket).
  • Cartilaginous (amphiarthroses): Slightly movable. Intervertebral discs, pubic symphysis.
  • Synovial (diarthroses): Freely movable. Types: ball-and-socket (hip/shoulder), hinge (elbow/knee), pivot (atlantoaxial), condyloid (wrist), saddle (thumb), gliding (carpals).

  • Right side: Deoxygenated blood → RA (tricuspid) → RV (pulmonic) → pulmonary artery → lungs.
  • Left side: Oxygenated blood from lungs → pulmonary veins → LA (mitral) → LV (aortic) → aorta.

4.2 Coronary Circulation

  • Right coronary artery: Supplies right atrium, right ventricle, SA/AV nodes (in most).
  • Left main coronary artery: Branches into:
    • Left anterior descending (LAD): anterior wall, septum.
    • Left circumflex (LCx): lateral/posterior wall.
  • Cardiac veins: Drain into coronary sinus → right atrium.

4.3 Cardiac Cycle & Conduction

  • Conduction pathway: SA node (pacemaker) → AV node (delay) → Bundle of His → Purkinje fibers.
  • ECG waves: P wave (atrial depolarization), QRS (ventricular depolarization), T wave (ventricular repolarization).
  • Cycle phases: Systole (contraction/ejection), Diastole (relaxation/filling).

5. Respiratory System — Airways & Alveoli

5.1 Upper & Lower Respiratory Tract

  • Upper: Nasal cavity, pharynx (nasopharynx, oropharynx, laryngopharynx), larynx (epiglottis, vocal cords).
  • Lower: Trachea → carina → right/left main bronchi → bronchioles → terminal bronchioles → respiratory bronchioles → alveolar ducts → alveoli.

6. Nervous System — Brain, Spinal Cord, Nerves

6.1 Brain Lobes & Functions

  • Frontal: Executive function, motor (precentral gyrus), speech (Broca's area — left).
  • Parietal: Somatosensory (postcentral gyrus), spatial perception.
  • Temporal: Auditory, memory (hippocampus), language comprehension (Wernicke's).
  • Occipital: Visual processing.
  • Cerebellum: Coordination, balance.
  • Brainstem: Midbrain, pons, medulla — vital centers (respiratory, cardiac).

7. Endocrine System — Glands & Hormones

7.1 Major Endocrine Glands

  • Hypothalamus: Releasing/inhibiting hormones, ADH, oxytocin (stored in posterior pituitary).
  • Pituitary (anterior): GH, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, prolactin.
  • Pituitary (posterior): ADH, oxytocin (released).
  • Thyroid: T3/T4 (metabolism), calcitonin (lowers Ca²⁺).
  • Parathyroid: PTH (raises Ca²⁺).
  • Adrenal cortex: Cortisol, aldosterone, androgens.
  • Adrenal medulla: Epinephrine, norepinephrine.
  • Pancreas (islets): Insulin (β cells), glucagon (α cells), somatostatin (δ cells).
  • Gonads: Testes (testosterone), ovaries (estrogen, progesterone).

9. Urinary System — Kidney & Nephron

9.1 Kidney Structure

  • External: Renal capsule, hilum (renal artery, vein, ureter).
  • Internal: Cortex (outer), medulla (pyramids), renal pelvis, calyces.

9.3 Urine Formation Steps

  1. Glomerular filtration: Blood pressure forces water/solutes through filtration membrane into Bowman's capsule (filtrate).
  2. Tubular reabsorption: Essential substances (glucose, amino acids, ions, water) moved from tubule to blood.
  3. Tubular secretion: Waste products (H⁺, K⁺, drugs) moved from blood to tubule.

9.4 RAAS (Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System)

  • Low BP → Renin released from juxtaglomerular cells → converts angiotensinogen → angiotensin I → ACE (lungs) → angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + aldosterone release (Na⁺/H₂O retention) → BP increases.

10. Reproductive System — Male & Female

10.1 Male Anatomy

  • Testes: Produce sperm and testosterone (in seminiferous tubules, interstitial cells).
  • Epididymis: Sperm maturation and storage.
  • Vas deferens: Duct for sperm transport.
  • Accessory glands: Seminal vesicles (fructose, prostaglandins), prostate (alkaline fluid), bulbourethral (lubrication).
  • Urethra: Common passage for urine and semen.

10.2 Female Anatomy

  • Ovaries: Produce ova, estrogen, progesterone.
  • Fallopian tubes: Site of fertilization; fimbriae capture ovum.
  • Uterus: Layers: perimetrium, myometrium (smooth muscle), endometrium (shed in menstruation).
  • Cervix: Opening to vagina.
  • Vagina: Birth canal, receives penis.
  • Mammary glands: Produce milk (lactation).
