2. Skeletal System — Bones & Joints

Divisions

  • Axial (80): Skull, vertebrae, ribs, sternum
  • Appendicular (126): Limbs, pectoral & pelvic girdles

Joint Types

  • Fibrous: Immovable (skull sutures)
  • Cartilaginous: Slightly movable (intervertebral discs)
  • Synovial: Freely movable (knee, shoulder)

3. Muscular System — Movement

Muscle Types

  • Skeletal: Voluntary, striated
  • Cardiac: Involuntary, striated, intercalated discs
  • Smooth: Involuntary, non-striated (viscera)

Major Muscles

  • Deltoid: Shoulder abduction
  • Pectoralis major: Chest flexion/adduction
  • Biceps brachii: Elbow flexion
  • Quadriceps: Knee extension
  • Hamstrings: Knee flexion

4. Cardiovascular — Heart & Vessels

Heart Chambers

  • Right atrium (receives deoxygenated blood)
  • Right ventricle (pumps to lungs)
  • Left atrium (receives oxygenated blood)
  • Left ventricle (pumps to body)

Valves

  • Tricuspid (right AV), Mitral (left AV)
  • Pulmonary (right ventricular outflow)
  • Aortic (left ventricular outflow)
    Blood flow: RA → tricuspid → RV → pulmonary → lungs → pulmonary veins → LA → mitral → LV → aortic → body

5. Respiratory System — Airways & Lungs

Upper Respiratory Tract

  • Nasal cavity, pharynx (naso/oro/laryngo), larynx (epiglottis, vocal cords)

Lower Respiratory Tract

  • Trachea → bronchi (right main wider, more vertical) → bronchioles → alveolar ducts → alveoli

Alveoli Structure

  • Type I pneumocytes: thin for gas exchange (95% surface)
  • Type II pneumocytes: produce surfactant (prevents collapse)
  • Alveolar macrophages: dust cells – phagocytic

7. Nervous System — Brain, Cord, Nerves

Central Nervous System (CNS)

  • Brain: Cerebrum (lobes: frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital), cerebellum, brainstem (midbrain, pons, medulla)
  • Spinal cord: Gray matter (horns), white matter (tracts)

Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

  • Cranial nerves (12 pairs)
  • Spinal nerves (31 pairs)
  • Autonomic: sympathetic (fight/flight), parasympathetic (rest/digest)

8. Endocrine System — Hormones & Glands

Major Glands

  • Hypothalamus: Releasing hormones, ADH, oxytocin (storage)
  • Pituitary (anterior): GH, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, prolactin
  • Pituitary (posterior): ADH, oxytocin (release)
  • Thyroid: T3/T4 (metabolism), calcitonin (↓Ca)
  • Parathyroid: PTH (↑Ca)
  • Adrenal cortex: Cortisol, aldosterone, androgens
  • Adrenal medulla: Epinephrine, norepinephrine
  • Pancreas (islets): Insulin (β), glucagon (α), somatostatin (δ)
  • Gonads: Testes (testosterone), ovaries (estrogen, progesterone)

Feedback Loops

    Negative feedback (thyroid):
    Hypothalamus (TRH) → Pituitary (TSH) → Thyroid (T3/T4) → inhibits TRH/TSH

    Positive feedback (oxytocin):
    Uterine contraction → more oxytocin → stronger contraction

9. Urinary (Excretory) System — Kidneys & Nephrons

Kidney Structure

  • Cortex (outer), medulla (pyramids), renal pelvis, ureter

The Nephron — Functional Unit

  • Glomerulus: Filtration of blood
  • Bowman's capsule: Collects filtrate
  • Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT): Reabsorption (glucose, AA, Na⁺, H₂O)
  • Loop of Henle: Countercurrent multiplier (water & salt reabsorption)
  • Distal convoluted tubule (DCT): Secretion (K⁺, H⁺), regulated by aldosterone
  • Collecting duct: ADH-regulated water reabsorption

Urine formation: filtration, reabsorption, secretion

RAAS (Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System)

  • Low BP → renin → angiotensin I → ACE → angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + aldosterone → Na⁺/H₂O retention → BP up

10. Reproductive System — Male & Female

Male Anatomy

  • Testes: Produce sperm & testosterone (seminiferous tubules, Leydig cells)
  • Epididymis: Sperm maturation & storage
  • Vas deferens: Duct for sperm
  • Accessory glands: Seminal vesicles (fructose), prostate (alkaline fluid), bulbourethral (lubrication)
  • Urethra: Common passage for urine/semen

Female Anatomy

  • Ovaries: Produce ova, estrogen, progesterone
  • Fallopian tubes: Site of fertilization; fimbriae capture ovum
  • Uterus: Perimetrium, myometrium, endometrium
  • Cervix: Opening to vagina
  • Vagina: Birth canal, receives penis
  • Mammary glands: Milk production
